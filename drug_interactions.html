Drug Interactions Reference BUILD #1

Drug Interactions Reference

High-yield drug interactions for hospital medicine. Focus on warfarin, DOACs, and immunosuppressants.

Interaction Severity Key

CONTRAINDICATED Avoid combination entirely
MAJOR Significant clinical effect; requires intervention
MODERATE May require dose adjustment or monitoring
MINOR/SAFE Minimal clinical significance

General Principles

  • Always check interactions when starting new medications in patients on anticoagulants or immunosuppressants
  • CYP450 enzymes mediate most drug interactions ‚Äì know the major ones (3A4, 2C9, 2D6, 1A2)
  • P-glycoprotein (P-gp) affects absorption and elimination of many drugs including DOACs
  • Renal function affects drug levels independent of CYP interactions

Warfarin Interactions

Warfarin is metabolized primarily by CYP2C9, with contributions from CYP3A4 and CYP1A2. It has a narrow therapeutic index making interactions clinically significant.

DOAC Interactions

DOACs (apixaban, rivaroxaban, dabigatran, edoxaban) have fewer interactions than warfarin but P-gp and CYP3A4 interactions are clinically important.

Metabolism: CYP3A4 (major), P-gp substrate

Immunosuppressant Interactions

Calcineurin inhibitors (tacrolimus, cyclosporine) and mTOR inhibitors (sirolimus, everolimus) have critical CYP3A4 and P-gp interactions. Levels must be monitored.

Metabolism: CYP3A4 (major), P-gp substrate. Narrow therapeutic index ‚Äì small changes cause toxicity or rejection.

Hunter Criteria (Most Sensitive/Specific)

In patient with serotonergic drug exposure, serotonin syndrome if ANY of:

  • Spontaneous clonus
  • Inducible clonus + agitation or diaphoresis
  • Ocular clonus + agitation or diaphoresis
  • Tremor + hyperreflexia
  • Hypertonia + temperature >38¬∞C + ocular or inducible clonus

Management

  • Stop all serotonergic drugs
  • Supportive care: IV fluids, cooling if hyperthermic
  • Benzodiazepines: For agitation, muscle rigidity, seizures
  • Cyproheptadine: Serotonin antagonist. 12 mg initial, then 2 mg q2h until improvement (max 32 mg/day)
  • Avoid: Physical restraints (increase muscle rigidity and rhabdo risk)
  • ICU admission for severe cases (hyperthermia >40¬∞C, seizures, respiratory failure)

Most cases resolve within 24-72 hours after stopping offending drugs.
