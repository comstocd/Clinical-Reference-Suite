üö´ Drugs to Avoid in CKD

‚ñº
CONTRAINDICATED or AVOID in severe CKD/ESRD:
  • Nitrofurantoin - Ineffective if CrCl <30 + neurotoxicity risk
  • Metformin - Lactic acidosis if CrCl <30
  • Meperidine - Normeperidine ‚Üí seizures
  • Morphine - M6G accumulation ‚Üí CNS/respiratory toxicity
  • Lithium - Accumulates, very narrow index
  • Gadolinium - NSF risk if GFR <30
  • Oral bisphosphonates - Avoid if CrCl <30-35
  • Colchicine (chronic) - Accumulates, myoneuropathy
  • NSAIDs - Worsen renal function, hyperkalemia, fluid retention
  • Dabigatran - Renally cleared, no reversal data in ESRD
USE WITH EXTREME CAUTION:
  • ACE-I/ARBs - Monitor K+ and Cr closely, start low
  • Spironolactone/Eplerenone - High hyperkalemia risk
  • TMP-SMX - Hyperkalemia, ‚Üëcreatinine
  • Aminoglycosides - Nephrotoxic + ototoxic, use if no alternative
  • Amphotericin B - Highly nephrotoxic
  • IV contrast - CIN risk, hydrate before/after
‚úÖ Safe alternatives in CKD:
  • Opioids: Fentanyl, hydromorphone (preferred over morphine)
  • Antibiotics: Ceftriaxone (no adjustment), doxycycline, azithromycin
  • DOACs: Apixaban (safe even in HD)
  • Antifungals: Echinocandins (no adjustment)
