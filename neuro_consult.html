📜 Relevant Neurological History

▼

Red Flag Headache Features — Rule Out Before Benign Diagnosis

  • Thunderclap — SAH until proven otherwise → LP if CT negative
  • New headache >50 years → GCA, mass lesion
  • Progressive worsening over days–weeks → elevated ICP, mass, SDH
  • Positional headache (worse supine) → elevated ICP vs. CSF leak
  • Fever + neck stiffness + headache → meningitis (do not delay LP/Abx for CT unless focal deficit)
  • HIV / immunosuppressed + headache → cryptococcal meningitis, CNS toxoplasmosis
  • Postpartum → CVT, PRES, reversible cerebral vasoconstriction syndrome

📊 NIH Stroke Scale (NIHSS)

▼
Stroke Treatment Thresholds
IV tPA (alteplase): Consider if NIHSS ≥4 (or disabling deficit) and ≤3h from LKW (up to 4.5h with extended criteria). Tenecteplase 0.25 mg/kg IV (max 25 mg) — non-inferior to alteplase, now preferred at many centers.
Mechanical thrombectomy: Large vessel occlusion (MCA/ICA/basilar), NIHSS typically ≥6, within 6h (up to 24h with DAWN/DEFUSE-3 criteria — mismatch imaging required).
NIHSS Total: 0 — No stroke symptoms

📷 Neuroimaging & Studies

▼

ASPECTS (Alberta Stroke Program Early CT Score) — for MCA territory

  • 10 regions scored (subtract 1 per region with early ischemic change): M1–M6, caudate, lentiform, internal capsule, insular cortex
  • ASPECTS ≥6: Favorable territory for thrombectomy
  • ASPECTS <6: Large established infarct — higher hemorrhagic transformation risk, discuss carefully

🧠 Assessment & Synthesis

▼

🚨 Neurological Emergencies — Confirm None Missed

  • ⚡Code Stroke / LVO: Last known well + NIHSS + CT/CTA → tPA / thrombectomy decision within minutes
  • ⚡Status Epilepticus: Seizure >5 min or ≥2 without recovery → Benzo → AED → anesthesia escalation
  • ⚡Bacterial Meningitis: Fever + AMS + meningismus → Abx + dexamethasone immediately; LP can follow if no focal deficit
  • ⚡SAH: Thunderclap HA → CT Head; if negative → LP at ≥6h (xanthochromia + RBC); CTA immediately
  • ⚡Increased ICP / Herniation: Cushing triad (HTN, bradycardia, irregular breathing) → HOB 30°, hyperventilate, mannitol/HTS, neurosurgery
  • ⚡Myasthenic Crisis: Bulbar/respiratory weakness → FVC, NIF monitoring; pyridostigmine hold; IVIG or plasmapheresis
  • ⚡GBS with Respiratory Compromise: FVC <20 mL/kg or NIF <−25 cmH₂O → elective intubation; IVIG or plasmapheresis
  • ⚡Wernicke Encephalopathy: Triad (ataxia / ophthalmoplegia / confusion) in any malnourished/ETOH patient → Thiamine 500 mg IV TID × 3 days before any glucose
