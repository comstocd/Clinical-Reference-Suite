🧪 Laboratory Data

▼

Calcium Interpretation

  • Corrected Ca = Measured Ca + 0.8 × (4.0 − Albumin)
  • High Ca + High PTH → Primary hyperparathyroidism (or tertiary)
  • High Ca + Low PTH → Malignancy, granulomatous disease, vitamin D toxicity
  • Low Ca + High PTH → Secondary hyperparathyroidism (VitD deficiency, CKD)
  • Low Ca + Low PTH → Hypoparathyroidism (post-surgical, autoimmune)

TFT Interpretation Pitfalls (Inpatient)

  • Sick euthyroid: Low T3 ± low T4 with normal/low TSH — do NOT treat if no clinical thyroid syndrome
  • TSH rebound (recovery phase): Transiently elevated TSH (3–20) — recheck outpatient
  • Dopamine / high-dose steroids: Suppress TSH — do not diagnose hyperthyroidism
  • TSH >20 + low free T4: True hypothyroidism — treat
  • Biotin supplements: Cause assay interference — hold 2–3 days before testing

Cortisol Interpretation

  • Random cortisol <3 µg/dL → strongly suggests adrenal insufficiency
  • Random cortisol >18–20 µg/dL → adrenal insufficiency unlikely (if critically ill, threshold may be higher)
  • Stimulated cortisol <18 µg/dL at 30 or 60 min → adrenal insufficiency
  • Low cortisol + high ACTH → primary adrenal insufficiency (Addison's)
  • Low cortisol + low ACTH → secondary/tertiary (pituitary/hypothalamic)

🧠 Assessment & Synthesis

🚨 Endocrine Emergencies — Confirm None Present

  • ⚡ Adrenal Crisis — Hypotension, N/V, hyponatremia, cortisol <3 µg/dL → Hydrocortisone 100 mg IV NOW
  • ⚡ Thyroid Storm (Burch-Wartofsky ≥45) — High-dose PTU/methimazole, iodine, steroids, beta-blocker
  • ⚡ Myxedema Coma — IV levothyroxine + hydrocortisone (always cover adrenals first)
  • ⚡ Pituitary Apoplexy — Hydrocortisone immediately; neurosurgery if vision loss
  • ⚡ Severe Hypercalcemia (>14) — Aggressive IVF, calcitonin, bisphosphonate
  • ⚡ Severe Hypocalcemia / Tetany — IV calcium gluconate (1–2 g over 20 min)
